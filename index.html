LEADERSHIP

I attended The University of Southern Mississippi with the intention of going to medical school. As a student I held numerous leadership positions including president of the American Medical Student Association, Vice President and Secretary of Alpha Epsilon Delta, a preprofessional health organization, member of the Student Government Association Judicial Board, and others.

I served as the Chaplain of my fraternity, Pi Kappa Phi, which involved organizing and directing our rituals, including initiation of new members. As pictured here, we were consistently recognized at our yearly national conference for exceptional performance in academics, membership retention, and most importantly, philanthropy.

RESEARCH

Throughout my years at USM, I contributed significantly to scientific research. I aided field research on endocrinology of cardinals, successfully transfected genes in an acquatic worm species using CRISPR-Cas9, and co-authored research studying the usage of bacteriophages to treat bacterial infections.

Pictured here, I had the pleasure of presenting my Honors Thesis, Phylogenetic Relationships of Salicaceae Based on Analyses of Nuclear DNA Data, at the yearly Association of Southeastern Biologists conference in Montgomery, AL.

CAREER TRANSITION

I had the pleasure of graduating magna cum laude with a B.S. in Biological Sciences alongside my high school best friend.

As much as I had enjoyed studying biology, I found myself longing to satisfy my drive to create. As such, I decided not to apply to medical school but instead attend App Academy, an intensive coding bootcamp, in order to pursue software development.
